About Us

Micro-credential Council, Inc. is a professional, non-stock, non-profit organization that brings together educators, researchers, institutions, industry partners, and policymakers committed to advancing micro-credentials and lifelong learning. While rooted in the Philippines, its membership extends to international partners and is open to individuals and institutions across Asia and beyond.

Mission and Vision

Vision

A dynamically and globally connected community advancing high-quality, interoperable micro-credentials for lifelong learning.

Mission

To empower educators, researchers, institutions, industry partners, and policymakers through collaboration, research, capacity building, offering, standards-driven innovation and certification in micro-credential development and recognition.

Purpose

The Council is established to advance micro-credentials as essential components of lifelong learning, skills development, and global workforce readiness. To fulfill this mandate, the Council shall:

1

Promote the growth, understanding, and responsible implementation of micro-credentials in education, training, and professional development;

2

Provide training and capacity building programs that equip educators, institutions, and industry partners with the competencies needed to design, deliver, and evaluate micro-credential programs;

3

Support micro-credential offerings by enabling the development, dissemination, and recognition of high-quality micro-credential models across diverse disciplines and sectors;

4

Foster research and advancement by encouraging studies, innovation, and continuous improvement in micro-credential practices, pedagogies, systems, and technologies;

5

Strengthen industry connections to ensure that micro-credentials remain relevant, responsive, and aligned with evolving labor market and workforce needs;

6

Ensure quality assurance by promoting alignment with national and international standards and frameworks, including those of government bodies, national qualification frameworks, and other global bodies;

7

Promote international collaboration by partnering with institutions across borders and harmonizing micro-credential systems to enhance global mobility and recognition;

8

Establish and maintain a registry of micro-credential programs, practitioners, and institutions to support transparency, interoperability, and shared recognition within the micro-credential ecosystem; and

9

Recognize, and certify micro-credential courses offered in the Philippines, and the World.
